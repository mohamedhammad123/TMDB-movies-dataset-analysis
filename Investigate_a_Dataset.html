#Creating a function to filter the data without the zero values for budget & revenue and getting the mean revenue for each genre
def filtered_data(dataframe):
    non_zero=Merged_df[(Merged_df.revenue>0)&(Merged_df.budget>0)]
    after_2010=non_zero[non_zero.release_year>2010]
    Mean=after_2010.groupby('main_genre').revenue.mean()
    return Mean

Conclusions¶

First of all , i wanted to show how the popularity of a genre would change over the years and i visualized this using a line chart for each genre showing the average population over the years.¶

And still with the genres data, i calculated the highest revenue for each genre in the last decade and visualized it using a bar chart so the difference between revenues for each genre be obvious.

Then,I sliced the data to get the top 10 movies that achieved the highest difference between movie budget and revenue and visualized it using a bar chart

And by knowing that creating a good movie not only depends on how its story or impact was great but also by how much money does the movie achieved so i analyzed the data to get the top 10 directors who succeded in both Revenue and Producing a good movie to watch, and i visualized the data using a pie chart to show the information i got and i think there were goof results.

Finally,I analyzed the data to get the correlation between vote average and high revenues, does every good movie get high revenue? Well, there was a positive correlation but it is not a matter we would assume as the highest movie in my analysis was Avatar and by far,and from my scatter visualization we can see that there are some exceptions.

We can also notice that after rating average with 8,the scatter was somehow down,so yes in general there is a positive correlation but this is not the only factor.

Limitations¶

The data was missing too many values in budget and revenue so i had to filter it to get practical results,this could impact the data negatively.

Having a column for critic reviews would have been very useful when comparing it with the average rating, but unfortunately that was not included

There is no currency mentioned in the dataset so the budget and revenue were in numerical values
